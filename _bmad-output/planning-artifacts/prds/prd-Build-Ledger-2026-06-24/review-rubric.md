# PRD Quality Review — Build Ledger

## Overall verdict

This is now a strong, thesis-driven capability spec whose two stated reasons-to-exist — the `build-ledger.json` contract and the Redaction Rules — are genuinely locked rather than aspirational. The re-validation confirms **all six prior findings are resolved**: the CRITICAL `mirror_view` leak path is closed at four independent points (schema, FR-6, FR-11, and a dedicated API-Contracts paragraph) with a test fixture that asserts the key's absence; the redaction contract is now whole-document and fail-closed; the `visibility` enum has been replaced by an orthogonal `display_tier` + `allowlisted` model that restores the addendum's three tiers and separates sensitivity from mechanism; and the two open items that sat on the invariants (Allowlist seed, Mirror View exposure) have been decided and moved to a "Resolved" subsection. The v1.5 additions (FR-13 line-level, SM-6 acceptance ratio, the forward-compatible `attribution` block) are cleanly fenced out of v1 scope and do not leak into §6.1. **Gate verdict: safe to green-light to architecture/build.** Residual issues are medium-or-lower and none block: a small cluster of judgment-predicate consequences a story author must operationalize, three still-open questions (none on a safety invariant), and minor Assumptions-Index roundtrip gaps.

**Delta vs prior review (1 CRITICAL + 5 HIGH):**
- ✅ **CRITICAL** — public schema could hold `retrospective.mirror_view` → **RESOLVED**. `mirror_view` removed from the public schema; routed to a separate `private/mirror.json`; absence is a machine-checkable consequence in FR-6 + FR-11 + the test fixture.
- ✅ **HIGH** — redaction contract scoped to repository objects only → **RESOLVED**. Replaced by a whole-document "Global redaction invariant" naming all five public blocks, asserted before publish, fail-closed.
- ✅ **HIGH** — `visibility` enum collapsed the three privacy tiers and conflated tier with mechanism → **RESOLVED**. `display_tier` (3 values: `public`/`redacted`/`aggregate_only`) is the sensitivity axis; `allowlisted` (boolean) is the orthogonal mechanism axis; the `redacted` tier and a `category` field are now representable.
- ✅ **HIGH** — Q4 (Mirror View exposure) was a privacy decision masquerading as an open question → **RESOLVED**. Decided ("no — private-only") and moved to §8 *Resolved*.
- ✅ **HIGH** — two open items (Q1 Allowlist seed, Q4) sat on the locked invariants → **RESOLVED**. Both moved to §8 *Resolved*; the Allowlist-empty default is now stated as a decision and enforced by FR-6.
- ✅ **HIGH (cluster 5th)** — the overlapping redaction-scope / visibility-enum / open-items-on-invariants cluster → **RESOLVED** as above; SM cross-refs were also made explicit (SM-1 now enumerates FRs and SM-2 now covers FR-8).

## Decision-readiness — strong

A decision-maker can act on this without guessing. Decisions are stated as decisions: §5 Non-Goals is firm ("Not a productivity score, ever"; the explicit refusal to reintroduce cut apparatus, now naming the exact rejected patterns — cost/token leaderboards, "Wrapped"-style recap, desk-toy counters). §8 has been restructured so the two questions that previously sat on the invariants now appear under **"Resolved (no longer open)"** with the decision stated, the rationale, and a pointer to where it is enforced — "Mirror View exposure → resolved: no — the Mirror View is private-only" and "Allowlist seed for v1 → resolved: none." This is the single biggest decision-readiness improvement over the prior cut: the contract and the open-questions list no longer contradict each other.

Trade-offs are named with what was given up, not smoothed to neutral: the complexity-scoring model stays cut because "a self-chosen weighting is an opinion a skeptic can discount" (§6.2); Cursor/Copilot acceptance APIs are "explicitly out, all versions" with the reason (org/admin-gated, not available to a solo user) rather than a vague defer. The `[NOTE FOR PM]` at §6.2 (the six-pack packs are "emotionally load-bearing for the Window; revisit first if timeline permits") sits at a genuine tension. The three remaining open questions are all real and none is a disguised decision: cost-source authority (Q1), representative-systems framing (Q2), and hosting/runtime (Q3, carrying an `[ASSUMPTION]` fallback). No rhetorical-question-with-answer-in-the-next-sentence pattern remains.

Feature prioritization follows the thesis — the hero is the Co-Authorship Split (§4.2, FR-3), volume is demoted by FR-2's testable "raw LOC is not the largest or first figure," and the cut list (§6.2) removes precisely what would dilute the thesis. The new v1/v1.5/Vision phasing strengthens coherence rather than fragmenting it: v1.5 (line-level + Acceptance Ratio) is sequenced "because the data already exists locally and the v1 schema is built forward-compatibly to absorb it" (§6.1.1), and Vision items (git-notes store, standing OTEL collector, multi-subject) are layered after. This is an argument with a roadmap, not a backlog with headings.

Success Metrics validate the thesis. SM-3 ("AI-provenance legible in under one minute") tests the provenance-first bet; SM-4 tests survive-inspection; SM-5 tests the Mirror bet; the new **SM-6** tests the honesty guard ("the Acceptance Ratio carries the AI-contribution story, not raw '% AI'"). The counter-metrics are load-bearing and correctly aimed — SM-C1 (token/cost not maximized, now naming the three rejected spend patterns and adding "the Acceptance Ratio itself is also not a maximization target"), SM-C2 (LOC not headlined), SM-C3 (session/repo counts not a score) — with the closing line "optimizing any of them turns the Evidence Ledger back into the very vanity artifact it exists to refute." No DAU/MAU activity-proxy tell. The prior SM cross-reference gap is fixed: SM-1 now enumerates FR-1…FR-12 explicitly instead of a fragile range, and SM-2 now validates FR-8 ("the published `build-ledger.json` they open is well-formed and schema-valid").

## Done-ness clarity — adequate

Every FR (FR-1…FR-13) carries a "Consequences (testable)" block, and the safety-critical ones are now exemplary. FR-6 is the strongest in the document: a whole-document redaction assertion ("**no object anywhere in the public `build-ledger.json`**" contains a name/path/branch/message/client name/secret/transcript), an explicit fail-closed rule ("if the assertion fails, the run does not publish"), and a concrete test fixture that "exercises all five public blocks … including a 'private' repository … produces a published file that contains none of the prohibited fields … and has no `retrospective.mirror_view` key." FR-3 now also closes the prior "or reproducible from" escape hatch — its consequence reads "**present on or linked from** the page so a reader can reconcile it directly," which is testable. FR-8's "validates against the documented top-level shape" and FR-5's "No raw transcript text appears anywhere" remain solid.

What keeps this `adequate` rather than `strong` is a residual cluster of consequences that rest on human judgment a story author must operationalize:

- FR-2: "No bare superlative appears; every superlative is qualified to its measure." Concrete in intent, but "bare superlative" is a copy-review check, not a deterministic test without an enumerated forbidden-token list.
- FR-11: "The Window View contains no self-flagellation and no aspirational claim." "Self-flagellation" is a human-judgment predicate; the companion clause ("every claim traces to a memlog entry or git evidence") carries the testable weight.
- FR-10 / §Platform: the target browser set is now pinned ("latest stable desktop Chrome, Safari, and Firefox") — the prior "current browser" looseness is fixed. Good.
- §Cross-Cutting NFRs Performance: "loads fast on a current desktop browser" — adjective-without-bound, but self-acknowledged via `[ASSUMPTION]` and deferred to the addendum.

These are editorial-judgment clauses, each paired with a hard clause, so "done" is recoverable per FR. The new FR-13 (v1.5) is well-specified for a next-phase FR — its consequences bind to the schema ("emitted under the multi-layer `attribution` representation … under a `schema_version` MINOR bump, never breaking a page built for v1"), to privacy ("only **aggregate** line counts … **no transcript or session-log text** is ever read into the public file"), and to the honesty guard (low-confidence remainder "labelled **'inferred'** and never merged into the measured figure").
